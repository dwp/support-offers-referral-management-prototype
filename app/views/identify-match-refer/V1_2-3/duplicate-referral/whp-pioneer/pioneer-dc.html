Pioneer - WHP Disability Condition

Overview

The Pioneer Work and Health Programme (WHP) helps people who face a range of complex barriers to finding work.

WHP advisers will support participants for up to 15 months by:

  • understanding their goals, strengths and experience
  • helping them to find and start work as soon as possible
  • providing ongoing in-work support

The opportunity is voluntary.

Eligibility

To be eligible they must not be claiming Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA).

They may be claiming:

  • Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Employment and Support Allowance Contributory (ESAC)
  • Incapacity Benefit (IB)
  • Income Support (IS)
  • Passport Incapacity Benefit (PIB)
  • Severe Disablement Allowance (SDA)
  • Training Allowance (TA)
  • no "out of work" benefits

They must also:

  • be disabled, as defined by the Equality Act 2010
  • be over 18 years old in England, or 16 years old in Wales
  • have the right to live and work in the UK
  • not be in any paid work, including zero hours contracts
  • not currently be on the WHP
  • not have been on the WHP before
  • not currently be on another national contracted opportunity
  • have recourse to public funds

Suitability

To be suitable they must:

  • have committed to the goal of finding work in 12 months
  • need extra support to find work

To take part in this opportunity, they must meet all the eligibility and suitability criteria.
